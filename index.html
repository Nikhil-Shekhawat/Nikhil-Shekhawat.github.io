About

I'm currently a Computer Science graduate student at the University of Central Florida.
I'm a part of the Parallel Programming lab advised by Dr. Sumit Kumar Jha where I'm working on verifying the designs of nano-scale circuit elements using GPGPUs for parameter space search. The work here has been accepted at GTC15
and has also been presented at International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium.
I got my bachelor's degree in Computer Science at IIIT - Delhi.
I strive to build technology that changes the world, by making people's lives easier.

Research

Avega: A Memristor Crossbar Simulator

With the demise of Moore's Law, there is a need for new computer architectures. This simulator validates the design of memristor based crossbar designs using GPGPUs. The project is being maintained by the Parallel Programming laboratory at UCF and the source will remain closed until published. For more details:

  • Extending Parallel Programming Education beyond Von Neumann Architecture
  • Avega: A Memristor Crossbar Simulator
